Interaction Design & Development (G103)

Overview

Interaction Design is an interdisciplinary field merging design skills such as user experience, interface development and graphic design with coding fundamentals to create meaningful experiences between the user and digital platforms such as social media, websites, wearables and mobile devices.

Throughout the Interaction Design and Development program, students will be expected to address such topics as mixed realities, immersive environments, usability, spatial relations and human factors. Students will engage in research, design, testing and group work to create original interactions in a way that is meaningful and practical.

Skils Learned

After completion of the program students will have the knowlexdge and experience to design digital products that solve user problems and serve client needs. Leverage foundational tools and techniques like user research, prototyping, testing, and more. Dive into human-computer interaction, information architecture, usability, and product management. After Completing This Program you will be able to:

  • Create meaningful digital experiences.

  • Explain your design process through sketches, wireframes, and presentations.

  • Prototyping and usability testing.

  • Build in a 3D workspace.

  • Make recommendations based on human computer interaction design guidelines to improve user experiences with interaction design.

  • Design a complex media project (interface, navigation, graphics, text treatment) using best practice design and development principles, and applying conceptual and theoretical frameworks.

  • Human Factors Training / HCI.


Educators

The educators at the School of Design represent the brightest user experience and interaction design talent from companies accross the globe. They bring in-depth experience from the field to the classroom each day, providing invaluable insights into succeeding on the job


Career Outcomes

Your experience at the School of Design will prepare you for your new career, giving you the confidence, knowledge, and experience needed to succeed. As part of the program, you will build an impressive portfolio of completed projects, showcasing the skills you have developed.


Exposure

The School of Design will provide 110,000 square feet of space for hands-on learning and collaboration, both among students and with industry partners. It will also be home to the Innovation Exchange, a research hub where students and businesses will team up and bring new ideas to market. The facility and the programming offered are designed to meet workforce demand by providing specialized, industry-identified skills training.
